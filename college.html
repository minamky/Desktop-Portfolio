Correlation Between Admission Rate & SAT Math

Shown Across Different Accreditors

University accreditors depend on campus location. While every graph shows a negative correlation, the strength of the correlation heavily varies.

The strongest correlation are found in schools that are accredited by the New England Commission on Higher Education at -0.87 while the weakest correlation is from the Southern Association of Colleges at -0.29.

Choropleth for Elite Colleges

Defined elite colleges as colleges that had a lower than 30% acceptance rate and had a higher than a 690 75th Percentile SAT score for both Math and Critical Reading. Grouped by State FIP Code, used the count function to find the number of elite colleges in each state, and merged this with the state GeoDataFrame on State Fip Code. Lastly, changed vmin to -1 so colleges with no elite colleges will still appear.

On the map, the darker blue states have relatively more elite colleges while lighter blue colleges have no elite colleges. There is a huge chunk of the United States in the top left that have no elite colleges.

Dot Plot of Flagship Colleges

Limited dots to only flagship colleges to compare the best colleges of each state. Created a geodataframe based on the flagship dataframe, set the coordinate reference system to the same as the state geodataframe, and changed parameters so the marks were more visible.

Lighter dots signify lower admission rates more present around coasts and populated states like Berkeley. Flagship colleges in midwest region still have relatively high admission rates like University of Kansas. This shows that not all the flagship colleges are competitive to get into, and if you want to go to a well-known competitive college, you may want to travel outside your original state.

There are so many higher institutions in the United States that many students feel overwhelmed when trying to find schools that they can get into. By using distance metrics, we are able to find colleges with similar statistics as a college that a potential applicant has already gotten into, allowing students to optimize their chances of getting admitted.

Machine Learning Models

Feature Selection

State, Accreditor, SAT Math 75th percentile score, Percent of freshmen submitting SAT scores, Completion Rate, and In State Tuition were the best features

Hypertuning

loss: absolute_error, max_leaf_node: 13, min_sample_leaf: 5 were the best parameters

Comparing Models to Find Lowest RSME

HistGradientBoostingRegressor: 0.140 RSME vs. RandomForestRegressor: 0.147 RSME

Applying Model to Stanford University
